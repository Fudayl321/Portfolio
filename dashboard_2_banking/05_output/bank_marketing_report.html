Data Pipeline

1

Data Source — UCI Bank Marketing

🔗 https://archive.ics.uci.edu/dataset/222/bank+marketing
Kaggle: kaggle.com/datasets/henriqueyamahata/bank-marketing
Portuguese bank telemarketing campaign, May 2008–Nov 2010 · 4,119 records · 20 features

2

Issues Found

25 duplicate rows — exact duplicates from data export error → dropped
60 nulls in age → median imputed per job group (preserves demographic signal)
30 nulls in duration → global median imputed (<1% missing)
'unknown' job → recoded using mode per education group

3

Feature Engineering

Created: age_group (bins) · contacted_before (pdays≠999) · duration_min (seconds→minutes) · high_euribor flag · month_num for sorting. Note: duration excluded from any model — it is data leakage (call length is only known after outcome).

4

Key Finding

Clients previously contacted with a successful outcome convert at dramatically higher rates. Retirement-age clients (>65) and students show strong conversion despite small volumes. May is the highest-volume month but not the highest conversion rate — March, September, and December outperform despite fewer calls.

SQL Queries Used

Subscription by Job

SELECT job, COUNT(*) total, SUM(subscribed) subscriptions, ROUND(AVG(subscribed)*100,1) AS sub_rate_pct, ROUND(AVG(duration_min),1) AS avg_call_min FROM bank GROUP BY job ORDER BY sub_rate_pct DESC

Previous Outcome Impact

SELECT poutcome, COUNT(*) total, SUM(subscribed) subscriptions, ROUND(AVG(subscribed)*100,1) AS sub_rate_pct FROM bank GROUP BY poutcome ORDER BY sub_rate_pct DESC
